# Doorway Risk Checklist For KIT SEO Pages

Use this before creating or publishing SEO landing pages.

## High-Risk Signs

A page is risky if it has several of these:
- nearly identical text to another page with only keyword replacements;
- page exists only to rank and not to help a real visitor;
- no unique commercial angle or user need;
- repeated boilerplate sections across many pages;
- thin content with keyword stuffing;
- artificial location/category permutations;
- no real navigation value;
- no distinct CTA or user path;
- no unique examples, comparisons, explanations, FAQs, materials, process notes, or limitations.

## Safer Page Criteria

A page is safer if it has:
- distinct search intent;
- distinct H1, title, description, and canonical;
- unique content structure;
- practical buying guidance;
- relevant images or visual rhythm;
- useful internal links;
- clear CTA;
- honest limitations and process details;
- FAQ that matches visible page content.

## Recommended Audit Output

For each page, return:
- risk level: low / medium / high;
- why;
- similar KIT pages that could overlap;
- required uniqueness improvements;
- internal linking recommendations;
- whether page should be published, merged, rewritten, or postponed.
